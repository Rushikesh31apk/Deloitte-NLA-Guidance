Process Details

Stage 1: Registration on HirePro

Fill application form with personal and academic details. Upload your resume. Make sure all eligibility criteria are met before applying.

Stage 2: Resume Review by HirePro

HirePro system reviews your resume automatically. Checks CGPA, course eligibility, and backlogs. Shortlisted candidates get email with test link.

Stage 3: Online Assessment on HirePro

Test includes 54 MCQs and 2 coding questions. Each section has its own time limit and cutoff. Must clear section-wise cutoff to proceed.

Stage 4: Interview Round

Combined technical and HR interview lasting 30-45 minutes. Tests your coding knowledge, concepts, communication, and attitude.

Stage 5: Final Selection

Verbal Offer letter sent within 2-4 weeks. Onboarding process starts with document verification and background check.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Ignoring Aptitude Preparation

Many students focus only on coding and ignore aptitude. Remember: You need to clear section-wise cutoff. If you fail aptitude, you're out even if your coding is perfect.

Mistake 2: Learning Answers Without Understanding

Just memorizing answers doesn't work. Interviewers ask follow-up questions. If you don't understand the concept, they will catch you easily.

Mistake 3: Poor Communication Skills

Not speaking clearly or confidently. Using too many filler words like "umm", "like", "basically". Even if your answer is correct, bad communication can reject you.

Mistake 4: Empty Resume

Resume with only academic details and no projects. Even small projects show that you have practical skills and initiative. Add at least 2-3 projects.

Mistake 5: Lying on Resume

Adding skills or projects you don't know. Interviewers will ask detailed questions about everything on your resume. If you can't explain, you'll be rejected immediately.

Mistake 6: Bad Body Language in Interview

Not looking at camera during virtual interview. Slouching or looking distracted. Messy background. Poor internet connection. These small things matter a lot.

Mistake 7: Not Practicing Mock Interviews

Going to real interview without practice. You need to practice speaking answers out loud. Record yourself and watch to improve.

Mistake 8: Not Reading Job Description

Not understanding what role you're applying for. Not knowing about Deloitte's work culture and values. Shows lack of interest.
